Aqeel Younas

I am Aqeel Younas, holding an MBA and a Master’s degree in Project Management and Business Engineering from CNAM Paris. My research lies at the intersection of entrepreneurship, strategy, and SME development, with a focus on how entrepreneurial leadership shapes firm competitiveness and firm performance in manufacturing SMEs.

In my master’s thesis, I conducted a quantitative study based on survey data from 200 manufacturing SMEs and examined structural relationships and mediation effects using PLS-SEM in SmartPLS. This research provides empirical evidence on SME growth and competitiveness in emerging economies, particularly within industrial clusters in Punjab, Pakistan.

I am currently preparing to pursue a PhD to deepen this research agenda, extend cross-country evidence, and develop theory-driven insights that support sustainable SME performance. I am especially interested in collaborative, data-driven research that connects academic knowledge with meaningful managerial and policy impact.

Research interests: Entrepreneurial leadership, SME competitiveness, firm performance, PLS-SEM, emerging economy entrepreneurship, and industrial clusters.

Research

My work examines how entrepreneurial leadership influences firm competitiveness and firm performance in manufacturing SMEs through quantitative structural modeling. I focus on emerging economies where SME development plays a critical role in economic growth, innovation, and employment generation.

Master’s Thesis

The Impact of Entrepreneurial Leadership on Firm Performance: The Mediating Role of Firm Competitiveness.

  • Cross-sectional survey of 200 manufacturing SMEs
  • Analysis using SmartPLS (PLS-SEM)
  • Direct and mediation structural relationships
