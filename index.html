Aqeel Younas

I hold an MBA and a Master’s degree in Project Management and Business Engineering from CNAM Paris. My research sits at the intersection of entrepreneurship, strategy, and SME development, with a focus on how entrepreneurial leadership shapes firm competitiveness and firm performance in manufacturing SMEs.

In my master’s thesis, I conducted a quantitative study using survey data from 200 manufacturing SMEs and tested direct and mediation relationships using PLS-SEM in SmartPLS. The study focuses on industrial clusters in Punjab, Pakistan, including Lahore, Sialkot, and Gujrat.

I am preparing to pursue a PhD to deepen this research agenda, extend cross-country evidence, and develop theory-driven insights that support sustainable SME performance and competitiveness.

Research interests: Entrepreneurial leadership, SME competitiveness, firm performance, PLS-SEM, emerging economy entrepreneurship, and industrial clusters.

Research

My work examines how entrepreneurial leadership influences firm competitiveness and firm performance in manufacturing SMEs through quantitative structural modeling. I focus on emerging economies where SME development plays a key role in jobs, innovation, and long-term growth.

Master’s Thesis

The Impact of Entrepreneurial Leadership on Firm Performance: The Mediating Role of Firm Competitiveness.

  • Design: Cross-sectional survey
  • Sample: N = 200 manufacturing SMEs
  • Method: PLS-SEM (SmartPLS), including mediation testing
