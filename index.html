About Me

Aqeel Younas

I am Aqeel Younas, a Master’s graduate in Project Management and Business Engineering from CNAM Paris. My research sits at the intersection of entrepreneurship, strategy, and SME development, with a focus on how entrepreneurial leadership influences firm competitiveness and firm performance in manufacturing SMEs.

In my master’s thesis, I conducted a quantitative study using survey data from 200 manufacturing SMEs and analyzed structural relationships and mediation effects through PLS SEM in SmartPLS. This work contributes evidence on SME growth and competitiveness in emerging economies, particularly within industrial clusters in Punjab, Pakistan.

I am now preparing to pursue a PhD to deepen this research agenda, expand cross country evidence, and develop theory driven insights that support sustainable SME performance. I am especially interested in collaborative, data driven research that links academic work with real managerial and policy impact.

Research interests: Entrepreneurial leadership, SME competitiveness, firm performance, PLS SEM, emerging economy entrepreneurship, industrial clusters.

Research

I study how entrepreneurial leadership shapes firm competitiveness and firm performance in manufacturing SMEs. My current work uses quantitative survey data and PLS SEM to test direct and mediated relationships.

Master thesis

The Impact of Entrepreneurial Leadership on Firm Performance: The Mediating Role of Firm Competitiveness.

  • Data: cross sectional survey, N = 200 manufacturing SMEs
  • Tool: SmartPLS (PLS SEM)
  • Model: EL to FP, and EL to FC to FP (mediation)
